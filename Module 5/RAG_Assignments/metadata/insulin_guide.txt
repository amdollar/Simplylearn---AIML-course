Insulin Administration Guide

Insulin is used to control blood glucose in people with diabetes. It must be injected under the skin (subcutaneously), commonly in the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm.

**Dosage**: Dosage is individualized based on blood glucose monitoring and physician recommendations.

**Storage**: Keep insulin in a refrigerator (2-8°C). Do not freeze. Unused insulin pens or vials should be discarded after 28 days once opened.

**Side Effects**: Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia), allergic reactions at the injection site, or weight gain.

Rotate injection sites to avoid lumps. If unsure about your dose, contact your doctor or diabetes nurse.
